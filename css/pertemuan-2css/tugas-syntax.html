Who is umar ibn khattab?


Umar ibn al-Khattab was the second Muslim caliph (634-644 CE), known for leading the early Islamic empire's expansion and for establishing principles of justice and equality under his rule. Born in mecca 584 CE, he was a strong, educated, and renowned wrestler and orator who initially opposed Muhammad before becoming a devout follower and close companion. As caliph, Umar's reign saw the conquest of Mesopotamia, Syria, Iran, and Egypt, transforming the Islamic state into a world power.


The story of how he became a muslim

Umar initially was a strong adversary of the Prophet Muhammad and Islam, even plotting to harm him. One day, he heard his sister reciting verses from the Quran, which deeply affected him. He then went to the Prophet Muhammad, declared his acceptance of Islam, and joined the community of Muslims.
